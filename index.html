About Me

Hello! My name is Joy, and I am an NLP Researcher at Tonative (Nigeria) and currently serve as Deep Learning Co-Lead at a href="https://x.com/AIplus_OAU" target="_blank">DSN OAU. My work focuses on developing language technologies for low-resource African languages, with a strong emphasis on speech recognition, machine translation, and healthcare applications. I am passionate about building culturally aware AI systems that bridge the gap between technology and African languages.

I hold a Bachelor’s degree in Linguistics and African Languages from Obafemi Awolowo University, Ile-Ife, Nigeria. During my undergraduate studies, I explored the intersection of linguistics and computational methods, which laid the foundation for my interest in low-resource NLP, tone modeling, and the application of AI in healthcare and education.

In 2025, I was awarded a grant by the Deep Learning Indaba to attend Deep Learning Indaba 2025 in Kigali, Rwanda, where I presented my work on ÒWE-YOR: Leveraging Transformer-Based Models for Yoruba Proverb Classification and received the Best Poster Award. I was also selected for the AfricaNLP Mentorship Program (ACL). These recognitions highlight my commitment to advancing NLP for African languages and contributing to the broader AI research community.

I am specifically interested in:

  • Preserving African languages through NLP and LLMs
  • Speech recognition and tone modeling for tonal languages
  • Machine translation for healthcare and education
  • Fair and culturally aware generative AI evaluation
  • Developing datasets and benchmarks for low-resource languages
  • AI applications for sustainability and social good
