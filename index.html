About Me

I am an AI/ML Developer and NLP Researcher with a background in Linguistics & African Languages (B.A., Obafemi Awolowo University). My work focuses on building language technologies for low-resource African languages, with a strong emphasis on speech recognition, machine translation, and healthcare applications.

In August 2025, I was awarded a grant by the Deep Learning Indaba to attend Deep Learning Indaba 2025 in Kigali, Rwanda, where I presented my work on ÒWE-YOR: Leveraging Transformer-Based Models for Yoruba Proverb Classification and received the Best Poster Award. I was also selected for the AfricaNLP Mentorship Program (ACL). Additionally, I have co-organized technical events as Deep Learning Co-Lead at Data Science Nigeria (OAU) and served as AI/ML Lead at Women in Data Science, Ile-Ife.

Professionally, I have contributed as an AI Developer at FriendnPal (Nigeria), a mental health tech organization, and as an ML Developer and Data Annotator at Kwame AI (Ghana), where I worked on speech-to-text technologies and dataset validation. My projects span across proverb classification, sentiment analysis, automatic essay scoring, and conversational AI systems.

I have been recognized with awards for my research contributions and leadership in AI. I am deeply passionate about preserving African languages through NLP and LLMs, and about applying AI to address challenges in healthcare, education, and sustainability.
